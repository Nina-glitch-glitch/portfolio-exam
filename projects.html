Overview

This portfolio was created as part of my UX and web development studies, with the goal of presenting my work in a clear, structured, and honest way. Rather than focusing solely on polished visuals, I wanted to highlight my process — how I think, why I make certain decisions, and what I learn along the way.

Problem

A portfolio is a first impression, and viewers decide within seconds whether to stay or leave. Research showed that clarity, simple navigation and fast understanding are crucial for engagement. My challenge was to design a portfolio that communicates who I am and how I work — clearly, quickly and without visual overload. It needed to feel professional and structured, yet still personal enough to build trust and make people want to explore further and eventually reach out.

Solution

I conducted qualitative interviews and a survey to understand what viewers expect from a portfolio. The findings shaped the structure, content and priorities of the design. Clear navigation, consistent storytelling and fast comprehension became the core design requirements. “Keep it simple” became the core design principle for this portfolio. When clarity increases, engagement follows.

Research

The research phase focused on understanding what employers expect from a portfolio. Through surveys and qualitative interviews, I identified recurring needs such as clarity, structure and transparency. Viewers want to quickly understand who the designer is, what decisions were made, and why.

These insights directly shaped the direction and priorities of the project.

Definition & Ideation

Based on the research insights, I translated the identified needs into clear design goals. Using tools like user stories, story mapping and empathy mapping helped define expectations from the viewer’s perspective.

This phase created a structured foundation and a roadmap for the rest of the project.

Design

The design phase focused on turning insights into a clear and intuitive structure before applying visual style. Wireframes were used to validate hierarchy, readability, and navigation — ensuring the viewer could quickly understand the content without distractions.

The layout was intentionally minimal to reduce cognitive load, using spacing and visual hierarchy to guide attention rather than decoration. Responsiveness was planned early to secure a smooth experience across desktop, tablet, and mobile.
